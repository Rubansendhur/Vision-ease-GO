Understanding Color Blindness

Color blindness, or color vision deficiency, is the decreased ability to see color or differences in color. It can impair tasks such as selecting ripe fruit, choosing clothing, and reading traffic lights.

Types of Color Blindness

Protanopia

Difficulty distinguishing between red and green colors. This is the most common type of color blindness.

Deuteranopia

Similar to protanopia, but affects the green cones. People with deuteranopia have difficulty distinguishing between red and green colors.

Tritanopia

Difficulty distinguishing between blue and yellow colors. This is a rare form of color blindness.

Causes and Prevalence

Color blindness is usually inherited and is more common in men than in women. It affects approximately 1 in 12 men and 1 in 200 women.
